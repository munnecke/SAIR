# Kolmogorov-Arnold theorem

A 1957 result stating that any continuous multivariate function can be represented exactly by a two-layer network of fixed width. [[Michael Mulligan]] showed empirically that standard MLPs and transformers spontaneously develop internal geometric structure predicted by this theorem during ordinary gradient-descent training — "zero row fraction" and "canonical alignment" both increase through training.
